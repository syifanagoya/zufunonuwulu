Geraldo Rivera FOX News Politics

Rivera currently serves as a news reporter and TV anchor on The FOX News Channel. He is the main host of The Geraldo Show airing on all FOX News Network. He first began his broadcasting career as a correspondent for the WABC news back in 1970. Geraldo’s work at the station earned him a Peabody Award and national attention because of his incredible reports.

Geraldo’s most memorable reports on ABC news include the neglect and abuse of patients with intellectual disabilities at Staten Island’s Willowbrook State School and Rockland County’s Letch worth Village. From there, he went on to appear as a co-anchor on the Nightline news programs on ABC. He then landed his first show known as The Mystery of Al Capone’s Vaults show which was billed as the unearthing of All Capone’s secret vaults.

In 1987, Geraldo began producing a nationally syndicated talk show called Geraldo. In 1994, Rivera joined CNBC news as an evening host of the Rivera Live talk show where he interviewed a wide range of guests and celebrities. Eventually, Geraldo left CNBC and joined FOX News as an investigative reporter before landing his own show in November 2011.
